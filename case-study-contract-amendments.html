Overview

Replacing two days of email threads and Excel files with a workflow that takes under an hour

Maersk's commercial operations teams negotiate thousands of contract amendments each year — rate changes, capacity adjustments, special surcharges. Before this project, the entire lifecycle lived in email chains and manually versioned spreadsheets. There was no audit trail, no single source of truth, and no way to know which version was current.

As lead designer on the amendments platform, I worked across operations, legal, engineering, and customer-facing teams to replace the spreadsheet process with a workflow embedded directly in the Maersk platform — end to end, without leaving the product.

Challenge

Two lost days per amendment cycle, 23% error rate, and version confusion at every handoff

The amendment process had never been designed — it had just accreted. An ops specialist would receive an amendment request, copy data into a spreadsheet template, send it by email for approval, wait for a reply, update the version, re-send. On average, a single amendment took 4.8 days from initiation to system entry.

The 23% first-submission error rate wasn't caused by carelessness. It was caused by the process itself: there was no validation layer, no confirmation of intent, and no way to see what had changed between versions. People were making decisions on stale data and didn't know it.

Service blueprint of the existing process revealed 11 handoff points — each a potential place for information to be lost, delayed, or duplicated.
Research

Three weeks of shadowing ops revealed the real bottleneck wasn't speed — it was confidence

I spent three weeks embedded with the operations team, shadowing amendment workflows in real time. I also ran structured interviews with 12 ops specialists and 5 customer-facing account managers.

The insight that changed the design direction: people weren't slow because the tools were hard to use. They were slow because they weren't confident in the information in front of them. Nobody trusted that the version they had was the current one.

→
The bottleneck was decision confidence, not data entry speed Ops specialists were spending 40% of their amendment time on verification calls — checking with colleagues whether the version they had was the right one.
→
11 handoff points where information could die The service blueprint revealed that each amendment passed through an average of 11 handoff points: email to spreadsheet, spreadsheet to approval, approval back to email, email to system entry. Each transition was a potential failure.
→
Approval chains had no memory Approvers couldn't see what had changed from the previous version. Every approval decision was made without a diff — people were approving documents they couldn't fully evaluate.
→
Both sides needed the same view Account managers and customers were working from different documents. The "current version" existed in two places and was frequently out of sync.
Process

Three concepts, one preference signal, and a hard constraint from legal that changed the design

After mapping the service blueprint, I designed three concepts and ran them through customer testing: (1) an enhanced email integration that kept the existing process but added structure; (2) a lightweight amendment tracker outside the main platform; (3) a fully embedded workflow inside the Maersk product.

Testing with 8 ops users showed a clear preference for option 3 — not because it was the fanciest, but because it was the only option where they didn't have to leave the platform they were already in. Context-switching was costing them more than they'd realised.

Step 01
Service blueprint
Mapped the full amendment lifecycle across five teams: ops, legal, account management, customer, and systems. Identified 11 handoffs and 4 points of systematic failure.
Step 02
Concept testing
Built three divergent concepts at varying fidelity. Ran 8 user sessions with ops specialists. Concept 3 (embedded platform workflow) achieved 7/8 preference across all sessions.
Step 03
Legal constraint sprint
Legal flagged a data-retention requirement mid-project: all amendment versions must be stored and auditable for 7 years. This became the version history feature — a constraint that turned into a feature users love.
Step 04
Pilot with two accounts
Ran a 6-week pilot with two enterprise accounts before full rollout. Iterated on the notification model and simplified the approval flow based on pilot feedback before general availability.
Solution

One place for every amendment: negotiate, compare, approve, done

The amendments workflow lives entirely within the Maersk platform. Both the ops team and the customer see the same current version, in real time. There is no email attachment, no manually renamed file, and no question about which version is current.

Version history with diff view. Every change is recorded and attributed. Approvers see exactly what changed between versions — field by field, before they approve.
In-line negotiation comments. Both parties can comment on specific fields without leaving the amendment. Conversations are attached to the record, not buried in email.
Approval chain with smart notifications. Each approver is notified when it's their turn — and only their turn. No flooding inboxes, no missed handoffs.
One-click bulk approvals. For standard amendments with no material changes, approvers can process multiple items at once — a feature that ops leads specifically asked for after the pilot.
Status timeline. A shared timeline visible to both the ops team and the customer shows where every amendment stands, without anyone needing to ask.
The amendments workflow: a single view showing current state, version history, and the approval chain — all in context of the existing contract.
Outcome

Half the turnaround time, a fraction of the errors, and a team that finally trusts the process

The platform launched to all enterprise customers in Q4 2024. Average amendment turnaround dropped from 4.8 days to 2.1 days — a 56% reduction. The first-submission error rate fell from 23% to 4%, eliminating most of the re-work loops that had been burning ops team time.

The version history feature — born from a legal compliance requirement — turned out to be the most appreciated part of the entire release. Ops specialists cited it as the single change that most improved their confidence in the process. Sometimes a constraint is a gift.

In the 6 months since launch, no amendment has been lost to an email thread. The ops team has processed 30% more amendments in the same headcount, without any increase in reported stress or overtime.
